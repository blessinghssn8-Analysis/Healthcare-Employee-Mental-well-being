# Healthcare-Employee-Mental-well-being
Healthcare Employee Well-Being, and Productivity Overview.
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of workforce wellness within an healthcare organization, i highlighted how stress, absences due to mental health, workplace factors, and departmental differences contribute to employee productivity and turnover intentions.

##The first section summarizes the general state of the workforce:

#Total Number of Employees
The total headcount sets the baseline for all subsequent metrics and comparisons across departments.

#Average Stress Level per Employee
This metric reveals the typical stress burden employees are experiencing. This is a higher-than-normal average, this signals operational pressures, understaffing, or difficult workplace conditions.

#Average Job Satisfaction
This shows the general sentiment of employees toward their role and work environment. Very low, this average is a baseline for departmental average. 

#Average Mental Health Absences
This highlights how frequently employees miss work owing to mental health related reasons. It is a critical early warning sign for burnout and declining well-being.

#Workplace Factors Impact Table
The table shows which workplace factors affect the highest percentage of employees such as workload, management support, work-life balance, recognition, and role clarity.

#Stress Level vs Burnout Frequency (Line Chart)
This chart reveals the relationship between employee stress and how often burnout symptoms occur. The upward movement indicates that the burn out frequency is essentially dependent on stress level. As the stress level increases, burn out frequency increases exponentially. 

#Job Satisfaction vs Stress Level vs Workplace Factor (Combo Chart)
This combined view highlights how WORK PLACE FACTOR behaves under different stress levels, while also showing It'sinfluence on Job satisfaction level. Job Satisfaction drops with increase Stress level, and more employee complain with Heavy work load, Work-life imbalance and others. 

#Burnout Frequency vs Number of Mental Health Absences.
This bar chart demonstrates how burnout materializes into measurable mental-health absences. The highh burnout often predicts higher absence rates, showing a tangible impact on productivity and staffing.
